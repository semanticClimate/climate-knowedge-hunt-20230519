Stern’s (2007) reference to climate change as “the greatest market failure in history” highlights that 11
damages inflicted by climate change are not properly costed in most economic decision-making. 12
Economic perspectives emphasise the value of removing fossil-fuel subsidies, and pricing emissions to 13
‘internalise’ in economic decision-making the ‘external’ damages imposed by GHG emissions, and/or 14
to meet agreed goals. Aggregate economic frameworks generally indicate carbon pricing (on principles 15
which extend to other gases) as the most cost-effective way to reduce emissions, notwithstanding 16
various market failures which complicate this logic.
