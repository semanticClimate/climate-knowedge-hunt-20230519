Strong differences remain in responsibilities for, and capabilities to, take climate action within and 4
between countries. These differences, as well as differences in the impact of climate change, point to 5
the role of collective action in achieving urgent and ambitious global climate mitigation in the context 6
of sustainable development, with attention to issues of equity and fairness as highlighted in several 7
chapters of the report (Chapters 4, 5, 14, 15, 17). 8
Innovation and industrial development of key technologies in several relevant sectors have transformed 9
prospects for mitigation at much lower cost than previously assessed (Chapters 2 and 6–12). Large 10
reductions in the cost of widely-available renewable energy technologies, along with energy efficient 11
technologies and behavioural changes (Chapters 5 and 9–11), can enable societies to provide services 12
with much lower emissions. However, there are still significant differences in the ability to access and 13
utilise low carbon technologies across the world (Chapter 4, 15, 16). New actors, including cities, 14
businesses, and numerous non-state transnational alliances have emerged as important players in the 15
global effort to tackle climate change (Chapters 13–16). 16
Along with continued development of concepts, models and technologies, there have been numerous 17
insights from both the successes and failures of mitigation action that can inform future policy design 18
and climate action. However, to date, policies and investments are still clearly inadequate to put the 19
world in line with the PA’s aims (Chapters 13, 15). 20

This Chapter introduces readers to the AR6 WGIII Report and provides an overview of progress and 26
challenges, in three parts. Part A, introduces the climate mitigation challenge, provides key findings 27
and developments since previous assessment, and reviews the main drivers for, and constraints against 28
accelerated climate action. Part B provides an assessment of the key frameworks for understanding the 29
climate mitigation challenge covering broad approaches like sustainable development and more specific 30
economic, political and ethical framings. Part C briefly highlights the role of governance for steering 31
and coordinating efforts to accelerate globally effective and equitable climate mitigation, notes the gaps 32
in knowledge that have been identified in the process of assessment and provides a road map to the rest 33
of the Report. 34
