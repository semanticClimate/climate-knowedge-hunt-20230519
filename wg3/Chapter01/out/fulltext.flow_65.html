FAQ 1.3 What is the difference between “net zero emissions” and “carbon neutrality”? 19
Annex I states that “carbon neutrality and net zero CO2 emissions are overlapping concepts” which 20
“can be applied at the global or sub-global scales (e.g., regional, national and sub-national)”. At the 21
global scale the terms are equivalent. At sub-global scales, net-zero CO2 typically applies to emissions 22
under direct control or territorial responsibility of the entity reporting them (e.g. a country, district or 23
sector); while carbon neutrality is also applied to firms, commodities and activities (e.g. a service or an 24
event) and generally includes emissions and removals beyond the entity’s direct control or territorial 25
responsibility, termed ‘Scope 3’ or ‘value chain emissions’ (Bhatia et al. 2011). 26
This means the emissions and removals that should be included are wider for ‘neutrality’ than for net-27
zero goals, but also that offset mechanisms could be employed to help achieve neutrality through 28
abatement beyond what is possible under the direct control of the entity. Rules and environmental 29
integrity criteria are intended to ensure additionality and avoid double counting of offsets consistent 30
with “neutrality” claims (see Annex I definitions of “Carbon neutrality” and “Offset” for detail and a 31
list of criteria). 32
While the term ‘carbon’ neutrality in this report is defined as referring specifically to CO
